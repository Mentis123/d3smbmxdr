Harness AI capability,
without the complexity

Adam Rappaport AI Specialist · Data#3
Connect

Most of the AI conversations I have with Australian customers right now sound the same. The strategy deck looks great. The board is bought in. Two or three pilots are running. None of them are in production. That gap, between a working proof of concept and an agent your business actually depends on, is the real problem. We call it pilot sprawl, and it has quietly become the biggest blocker to AI value in the enterprise.

So we built something different at Data#3. Not another platform. Not another framework deck. An operating model. The AI Factory is Microsoft AI specialists running with you on a sprint cadence, in dedicated squads assembled for the phase you're in. Six operational phases parented under three executive ones (Envision, Transform, Operate). Different phase, different squad — what's persistent is the operating model, the methodology, and continuity of engagement leadership across the lifecycle. Clarity compounds phase by phase before you commit to build. So you commit to production with clarity, not hope.

Capability you subscribe to. Not a project you scope.

The infographic below walks through the whole operating model. Six phases, five service tiers, one operating model. From a three to four week Lean Sprint that proves a single use case, through a Factory Pod that productionises 1–4 use cases, all the way to a fully managed AI capability on a rolling commit. Squads are how we deliver; the capability is what you buy. Move past pilot sprawl. Get to production.

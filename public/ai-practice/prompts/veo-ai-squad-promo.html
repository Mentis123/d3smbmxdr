Rules baked into every prompt Read once

These are already inside each shot's prompt. Listed here so you understand why they're there.

  • 8-second clips, 16:9 cinematic — Veo 3.1 default. One dialogue line per clip (Veo lip-syncs best when the line fits in one natural breath).
  • Dialogue in double quotes with explicit speaker framing — e.g. She says, "...". Veo treats anything in quotes as speech to render; everything else is description.
  • Audio labelled explicitly — Audio: for the soundscape, SFX: for punctuation. Veo syncs audio to visuals more reliably when the two are separated.
  • Lip-sync kept tight — every speaking shot frames at medium close-up or tighter with clearly enunciates, visible mouth movement baked in. One speaker per clip.
  • Character descriptions are inline — every shot that features the squad carries the full appearance description of each squad member inside the prompt itself. No cross-referencing. Copy-paste and go.
  • Shared negative prompt — every shot ends with: no subtitles, no captions, no on-screen text, no watermark, no music (unless the shot specifies), no cartoonish style.
  • Data#3 lockup is NOT generated by Veo — Shot 8 is a clean plate. Composite the lockup with cyan superscript # in After Effects over the plate.

Iteration tips If a shot misses

  1. Lip-sync drifting? Tighten the camera to a closer close-up, cut the dialogue to under 6 words, add "clearly enunciates, visible mouth movement, one speaker only, sync mouth to audio" explicitly inside the prompt.
  2. Wrong city / wrong building (Shot 7)? Veo's grasp of MCEC specifically is patchy. Reinforce with "Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre, South Wharf Melbourne Australia, distinctive angular steel and glass facade, Yarra River visible, iconic Melbourne architecture". If still wrong, accept a plausible modern convention centre and add a Melbourne establishing cutaway between 6 and 7 (trams, Yarra, cityscape).
  3. Crowd scale not reading? Add "thousands of people, deep crowd extending far beyond frame, dense packed bodies" and lift the crane higher. If still thin, generate twice and stack takes in edit to double the apparent density.
  4. Squad faces drifting shot-to-shot? Veo 3.1 drifts on identity across generations. Strongest fixes: (a) the character descriptions already baked into Shots 4, 5, 6 are your anchor — keep them verbatim, (b) use Veo 3.1's reference-image / Ingredients feature with a still from Shot 5 as the identity anchor for Shots 4 and 6.
  5. Tram bell / Australian ambience not registering? Explicitly add "Melbourne tram bell (Yarra Trams W-class, distinct metallic ding-ding)" and "Australian outdoor ambience".
  6. Wardrobe inconsistency? The cyan accent is the squad's visual anchor across all shots (lanyards, laptop stickers, a single rim-light). If it's drifting, add "subtle cyan accent in wardrobe or props" to the shot.

Pillar Post · ~900 words · Week 1 Tuesday

It’s the Windows 97 moment for AI — and the buying model has to change too

By Scott Gosling · Microsoft Practice Manager, Data#3

The five beats

  1. Win97 wasn’t about an OS. It was the moment computing stopped being a project and started being an operating reality. Office 97. The browser. Email-by-default. Companies that treated it as “an IT thing” got eaten.
  2. AI is doing it again, faster. Microsoft’s data: 82% of leaders expect agents in 12–18 months. MIT: 95% of GenAI projects fail. Both true at once.
  3. The reason for the failure rate isn’t models or data. It’s the buying model. Customers are still buying AI like a 2018 ERP project — scope, SOW, fractional resources, 9-month plan.
  4. We’re moving with it. Data#3 is shifting AI services to capability-as-a-service. You don’t buy hours. You buy a team, by the sprint, with a defined outcome.
  5. Reading list. Links to the rest of the rain — eight posts answering the practical questions this raises.

05 — Two angles to push back on Amanda

Where I’d argue with the brief, respectfully.

The original ask was “a Knowledge Centre post or series of posts.” If we want this to land — not get buried — two things matter more than the brief reads.

One-and-done won’t beat the noise.

Microsoft’s partners are out-publishing us 10:1 on AI. A single article gets indexed and forgotten. A spine gives sales a reusable library — that’s the bigger prize than any single post.

The hook is “speed to value with proof” — not “new model”.

Mark nailed this in the call. Every post must clear the “so what?” bar in the first paragraph. Lead with methodology → we lose. Lead with “this customer went live in 10 working days and here’s what we shipped” → we win.

06 — The production model

Same harness we already use. The way we make it is the proof.

The production model for the rain is the model we’re selling. Squad + sprint + Claude. Scotty’s pillar can literally say so.

How each post gets made
  • Drafting — I draft each post with Claude against the d3-brain vault. 80% there in an afternoon per piece.
  • Voice ownership — the named author edits to their voice before publish. Non-negotiable. Ghost-written posts under Mark’s or Richard’s name read flat — they’ll know.
  • Living drafts — ship as inline-editable HTML (this page’s pattern). Amanda, the named author, and reviewers edit in-browser. NeonDB-backed. Cuts review cycle from days to hours.
  • Proof point built in — we’re using the squad model to ship the campaign about the squad model. The pillar can say “this very page was built this way” with a link to this draft.

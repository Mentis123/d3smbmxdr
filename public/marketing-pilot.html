03  /  05  ·  the workflow

Eight stages. Five of them human gates.

The agent does the structural lift in the middle — proposes the beats, drafts v0.1, threads the KB links. Humans bracket it: customer questions in, SME ownership, voice edit, publisher, marketing. The signature stays human.

01
FAQs
Twenty questions the customer actually asks — the KB layer. Sourced from sales, not invented.
02
SMEs identified
Each FAQ gets a primary owner, support, and reviewer. Named voices, not anonymous content.
03 · AI
Agent proposes 5-beat
First sketch — five beats that answer the question, drawn from existing FAQs. Hours, not days.
04 · AI
Workshop the 5-beat
Refine with the SME until every beat earns its FAQ link. The argument before the prose.
05 · AI
Agent drafts v0.1
Prose grown from the beats, not from a blank page. Structured first draft.
06
SME edits to voice
The author writes themselves back into the post. Non-negotiable. Ghost-written posts read flat.
07
Publisher approval
Jared (or assigned editor) signs off. Editorial governance, same bar as today. No back doors.
08
Marketing review
Amanda layers the “why D#3” CTA, brand alignment, KB deep-links. Marketing in the loop on every piece.
Human gate AI-assist zone Five of eight gates are human. The agent does the lift in the middle.
04  /  05  ·  what we’re piloting

What success looks like.

Three testable outcomes. If all three land, we have a workflow other practices can adopt. If any of them break, we’ve learned cheaply — and the byline still belongs to a human, not an algorithm.

Outcome 01
Time-to-published collapses.

Idea → published byline in under two weeks per piece, vs. the current six-to-ten-week window. The compression comes from the AI draft step, not from cutting any human gate.

Outcome 02
Marketing workload reshapes.

Amanda spends ~1 hour per piece on the CTA + brand layer instead of 4–6 hours rewriting. Same governance, less typing. Capacity freed up for landing-page work and the bigger campaign motion.

Outcome 03
Voice fidelity holds.

Author and reader both confirm the byline reads like the named author. Internal voice-check before publish, customer feedback after. If a piece reads like Claude, it doesn’t ship.

Bonus: a reusable workflow template the security, modern work, and analytics practices can apply for their own thought-leadership campaigns — without re-inventing the operating model each time.

05  /  05  ·  non-negotiables

What doesn’t change.

If any of these four bend, the pilot stops. They’re what makes “AI-augmented” defensible — from a brand standpoint, an ethics standpoint, and a customer-trust standpoint.

01
The byline is the human’s. The named author edits to their own voice before publish, every time. AI never signs anything. Ghost-written posts read flat — readers know.
02
Marketing is in the loop. Every piece flows through Amanda for the “why D#3” CTA layer and brand alignment. No piece bypasses marketing — ever.
03
Transparency about the workflow. We say openly that AI helped draft, then the author finished. Customers value the honesty more than they punish the disclosure. This is also what positions us to talk credibly about responsible AI.
04
Governance unchanged. Jared editorial review, data3.com Knowledge Centre publishing standards, sign-off chain identical to current. The AI is in the draft step — not in the approval chain.
